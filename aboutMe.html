"A designer is a problem solver"

And Bay Area native, Vanessa studied for a degree in Psychology (B.S.) before deciding upon her current major, Design: Visual Communications (B.S.) at San Francisco State University. As she entered the world of design, she began to notice the ethics of the user experience were generally founded on "user-centered" designs rather than "human-centered" designs. She began to take the foundations she had in Psychology and applied it to her research and designs. Ultimately, her goal is to huamnize and evolve the visual language of a product.

Vanessa enjoys readings such "The Art of Thinking Clearly" (Rolf Dobelli) as well as "Minds, Brains, Computers: An Historical Introduction to the Foundations of Cognitive Science" (Robert Harnish)
